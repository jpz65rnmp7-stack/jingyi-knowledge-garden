# Stoicism and Daoism Discovered the Same Truth — 2000 Years Apart

## Core Thesis

Epictetus said: "Some things are within our control, others are not." Laozi said: "Wu wei" — not inaction, but action in the right place. Two thinkers separated by 7000 kilometers and 400 years arrived at the same operating system for the human mind.

---

## The Control Dichotomy

| You Control | You Don't Control |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Your judgments | Others' opinions |
| Your actions | The weather |
| Your attitude | The economy |
| Where you place attention | Your height, looks, background |

If you only claim what's truly yours — no one can hurt you.

---

## Modern Science Caught Up After 2000 Years

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy's core premise: "It's not events that disturb you, but your judgments about them." Epictetus said this in the 1st century AD.

Stoicism isn't about suppressing emotions. Daoism isn't about doing nothing. Both are about **directing your energy toward what you can change, and releasing what you can't.**

---

## The 3-Second Rule

Anxiety hits → pause one second → ask: "Is this within my control?" Yes → act. No → release. Not because you don't care. Because your energy is finite.

> The strongest people aren't those who control everything. They're those who know exactly what to control and what to release.
