# Your Brain Has Two Operating Systems — You're Using the Wrong One 99% of the Time

## Core Thesis

Kahneman discovered the brain's most alarming bug: we have two thinking systems, but 99% of decisions use the unreliable one. System 1 is lightning-fast but riddled with bias. System 2 is painfully slow but accurate. The problem isn't having two systems — it's not knowing when to switch.

---

## System 1 vs System 2

| | System 1 | System 2 |
|------|------|------|
| Speed | Instant (automatic) | Slow (effortful) |
| Energy | Low | High |
| Function | Pattern recognition, intuition | Logic, calculation |
| Usage | 99% | 1% |

You're not lazy — System 2 consumes too much energy, so the brain evolved a "use sparingly" strategy.

---

## System 1's Fatal Flaws

- **Confirmation bias**: Only seeing evidence that supports your view
- **Availability bias**: Recent events feel more important
- **Anchoring**: The first number you hear shapes all subsequent judgments
- **Loss aversion**: Losing $100 hurts more than gaining $100 feels good

Wealth builders must learn to recognize when System 1 is running the show.

---

## How to Activate System 2

- **Invert**: First assume you're wrong, then look for evidence
- **Zoom out**: What does this decision mean in 10 days? 10 months? 10 years?
- **Calculate**: Don't go by feeling. Calculate expected value.

> Charlie Munger: "Invert, always invert."
