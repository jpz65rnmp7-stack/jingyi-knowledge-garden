# Three Ways to Spot an NPC

## Core Thesis

NPCs aren't virtual characters — they're people who've never examined their own life script. Physically alive, consciousness never booted up. Three fatal tells reveal them.

---

## Tell #1: Only Three Scripts

NPCs are predictable. Their response to any situation is one of three:

| Script | Trigger | Auto-Response |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Victim | Setback | "It's everyone else's fault" |
| Judge | Difference | "You're doing it wrong" |
| Avoider | Choice | "I'll deal with it later" |

Real players create new responses. NPCs only access pre-installed programs.

---

## Tell #2: Never Upgrades Their Cognitive OS

NPCs share one trait: **they think they already know.** Mention "information asymmetry" — "Oh, that's just information gap." But they've never studied arbitrage models. They confuse labeling a concept with understanding it.

---

## Tell #3: Fear of Uncertainty

The NPC's operating system is **certainty-seeking.** Hate change → maintain status quo. Hate risk → never try. Hate conflict → never confront. But awakening means understanding: uncertainty isn't a threat — it's just another name for opportunity.

---

## Self-Test

When was the last time you changed your mind about something important? If you can't answer — you might be running on NPC mode.
